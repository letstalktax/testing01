2. Tax Implications

2.1. Value Added Tax (VAT)

The financial statements mention 'VAT payable' under Accrued expenses and other payables (Note 15). This indicates that Gourmet Gulf Co.LLC is registered for VAT in the UAE and is compliant with VAT regulations. The VAT payable amount is relatively small (AED 35,264 in 2023 and AED 759 in 2022), suggesting regular VAT settlements. Continued compliance with UAE VAT regulations, including accurate record-keeping and timely filing of returns, is essential.

2.2. Corporate Tax (CT)

The UAE introduced Corporate Tax effective for financial years starting on or after 1 June 2023. Given that Gourmet Gulf Co.LLC's financial year ends on 31 December, the financial year 2023 will be subject to UAE Corporate Tax. The reported net loss for 2023 might result in no corporate tax liability for this period. However, it is crucial for the company to:

  • Ensure proper registration for Corporate Tax.
  • Maintain detailed records as per CT law requirements.
  • Understand the implications of tax losses and their carry-forward provisions under UAE CT law for future profitability.
  • Seek expert advice to optimize tax planning and ensure full compliance with the new CT regime.

2.3. Withholding Tax

Based on the provided documents, there is no explicit mention of transactions that would immediately trigger withholding tax. However, if Gourmet Gulf Co.LLC makes payments to non-residents for services, royalties, or interest in the future, they may be subject to UAE withholding tax. The company should be aware of withholding tax obligations and ensure compliance as applicable.
