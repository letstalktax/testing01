3. Tax Implications

Based on the financial statements for the year ended 31 December 2023, the following tax implications are noted:

3.1. Corporate Tax

The UAE introduced corporate tax on business profits exceeding AED 375,000, effective for financial years starting on or after 1 June 2023. However, GOURMET GULF CO.LLC reported a net loss for 2023. Therefore, based on the reported financial results, there is no corporate income tax liability for the year 2023 as profits are below the taxable threshold and in fact, a loss was incurred.

3.2. Value Added Tax (VAT)

The presence of 'VAT payable' in the accrued expenses indicates that the company is registered for VAT and is likely compliant with VAT regulations in the UAE. Continued compliance with VAT obligations, including accurate record-keeping and timely filing of returns, is essential.

4. Financial Findings and Compliance Recommendations

4.1. Going Concern Assessment

The financial statements highlight a 'going concern' uncertainty due to accumulated losses and negative equity. While management expresses confidence in shareholder support, a robust plan to improve profitability and generate positive operating cash flows is crucial to ensure the company's long-term viability. Shareholder commitment should be formally documented and readily available.

4.2. Related Party Transactions

Significant balances and transactions with related parties are evident. It is imperative to ensure that all related party transactions are conducted at arm's length and are fully disclosed in accordance with IAS 24 - Related Party Disclosures. The nature and terms of these transactions should be reviewed to ensure compliance and fairness.

4.3. Cash Flow Management

The significantly negative operating cash flow in 2023 is a major concern. Management should conduct a thorough review of operational efficiency, cost management, and revenue generation strategies to improve cash flow from operations. The unusual 'Net movement in accumulated loss' within financing activities in the cash flow statement needs clarification and careful examination.

4.4. IFRS Compliance and Audit

The financial statements are prepared in accordance with International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS). Continued adherence to IFRS, including staying updated with new and amended standards, is necessary. Maintaining an independent audit by Spicer Pegler Accounting is a positive step towards ensuring the reliability and credibility of the financial statements.

5. Conclusion

GOURMET GULF CO.LLC shows signs of improved gross profitability in 2023, but faces significant challenges related to revenue decline, net losses, negative operating cash flow, and going concern uncertainty. Focus on improving operational efficiency, managing related party transactions transparently, and addressing the going concern issue are critical for the company's future financial health and compliance.
