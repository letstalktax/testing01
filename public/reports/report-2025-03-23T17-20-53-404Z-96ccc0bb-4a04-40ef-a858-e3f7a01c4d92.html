  3. Related Party Loan Review: Review the nature and purpose of related party loans and assess potential restrictions on interest deductibility under Article 31.

Analysis: Note 15 indicates 'Accrued expenses and other payables' of AED 857,786. Accrued expenses are generally deductible if they relate to bonafide business expenses and are compliant with the accrual basis of accounting. However, deductibility is contingent on meeting the conditions outlined in Article 28 of the UAE Corporate Tax Law, including being wholly and exclusively for business purposes and not being capital in nature.

Potential Corporate Tax Adjustments:

  • Non-deductibility of Non-Business Expenses: Accrued expenses that are not directly related to business operations or do not meet the 'wholly and exclusively' criteria may be disallowed.
  • Lack of Supporting Documentation: Insufficient documentation to substantiate the business nature and deductibility of accrued expenses may lead to disallowance.

Action Required:

  1. Expense Verification: Review the nature of significant accrued expenses and ensure they are genuinely business-related and deductible under Article 28.
  2. Documentation Review: Ensure adequate documentation exists to support the deductibility of all accrued expenses, including invoices, contracts, and evidence of business purpose.
  3. Accrual Policy Review: Review the company's accrual policy to ensure it aligns with the accrual basis of accounting and UAE Corporate Tax requirements.

5.3.5 Loss Carry Forward

Analysis: Gourmet Gulf Co LLC reported a net loss for the year ended 31 December 2023 and has accumulated losses carried forward. Article 37 of the UAE Corporate Tax Law allows for the carry forward of Tax Losses to offset future Taxable Income, subject to conditions including a 75% limitation on utilization in any given Tax Period and continuity of ownership (Article 39).

Potential Corporate Tax Implications:

  • Future Tax Relief: The carried forward losses can potentially reduce future Corporate Tax liability when the company returns to profitability.
  • 75% Utilization Limit: Loss utilization in future profitable years will be limited to 75% of the Taxable Income in that year.
  • Ownership Continuity: Changes in ownership may restrict the ability to carry forward and utilize Tax Losses under Article 39.

Action Required:

  1. Loss Tracking: Maintain detailed records of accumulated Tax Losses and ensure accurate tracking of carry forward amounts.
  2. Utilization Planning: Incorporate loss carry forward into future tax planning to optimize tax efficiency when the company becomes profitable.
  3. Ownership Monitoring: Monitor any potential changes in ownership structure and assess the impact on loss carry forward eligibility under Article 39.
