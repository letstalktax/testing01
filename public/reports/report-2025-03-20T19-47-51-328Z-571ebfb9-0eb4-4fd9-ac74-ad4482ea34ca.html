4. Tax Implications and Considerations

4.1. UAE Corporate Tax

The UAE introduced a Corporate Tax regime effective for financial years starting on or after 1 June 2023. While Gourmet Gulf Co.LLC is currently operating at a loss, which may shield it from corporate tax in the short term, it is crucial to consider future profitability and tax planning. If the company becomes profitable in future years and exceeds the taxable profit threshold, it will be subject to UAE Corporate Tax.

4.2. Value Added Tax (VAT)

As a restaurant business in Dubai, Gourmet Gulf Co.LLC is likely subject to UAE VAT. The financial statements mention "VAT payable" (Note 15), indicating VAT registration. Compliance with VAT regulations, including accurate record-keeping, timely filing of returns, and proper VAT collection and remittance, is essential. The revenue figures presented are likely inclusive of VAT, and the company needs to ensure proper VAT accounting.

4.3. Related Party Transactions and Transfer Pricing

The financial statements reveal significant transactions and balances with related parties (Note 14). These include substantial 'Due from related parties' and 'Due to a related party' balances, as well as sales and direct expenses with related parties. These transactions must be conducted at arm's length to comply with transfer pricing regulations. It is recommended that Gourmet Gulf Co.LLC maintains thorough documentation to support the arm's length nature of these related party transactions, especially given the significant amounts involved.

4.4. Withholding Tax

While not explicitly detailed in the provided information, payments to non-resident related parties (e.g., Azkadenya - Qatar) may be subject to UAE withholding tax on certain types of income, if applicable under UAE tax laws and any relevant Double Tax Avoidance Agreements. 5.1. Going Concern Uncertainty

Note 2.4 highlights a "Going Concern" issue. The company has incurred significant net losses and has negative equity. This reliance on shareholder funding indicates financial vulnerability and needs to be carefully monitored and addressed.

Administrative and general expenses are substantial (AED 16,695,988 in 2023). A detailed review of these expenses is recommended to identify potential cost optimization opportunities. Rent and salaries & benefits are major components of these expenses (Note 18).

The company's positive overall cash flow in 2023 is primarily driven by financing activities, specifically a "Net movement in accumulated loss" of AED 138,097,508 (Statement of Cash Flows). This reliance on external funding is not sustainable in the long term.

6. Compliance Recommendations

6.1. Corporate Tax Preparedness

Even with current losses, Gourmet Gulf Co.LLC should proactively prepare for UAE Corporate Tax. This includes understanding the tax regulations, maintaining proper accounting records in compliance with tax requirements, and monitoring future profitability to ensure timely tax compliance when applicable.

6.2. VAT Compliance Review

Conduct a thorough review of VAT compliance procedures to ensure accurate VAT accounting, timely return filing, and proper VAT collection and remittance. Verify that revenue recognition and VAT calculations are aligned with UAE VAT regulations.

6.3. Transfer Pricing Documentation

Given the significant related party transactions, it is strongly recommended to prepare and maintain robust transfer pricing documentation. This documentation should justify the arm's length nature of pricing and terms for all related party transactions to mitigate potential transfer pricing risks and scrutiny from tax authorities.

6.4. Going Concern Action Plan

Develop a comprehensive action plan to address the going concern uncertainty. This plan should include strategies to improve profitability, manage expenses, enhance cash flow from operations, and reduce reliance on shareholder funding. Regular monitoring of financial performance and communication with shareholders regarding the going concern situation is crucial.

6.5. Expense Management and Optimization

Implement measures to review and optimize administrative and general expenses. Focus on areas such as rent negotiation, operational efficiency improvements, and cost-saving initiatives to enhance profitability.

6.6. Financial Reporting and Audit

Continue to adhere to International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS) for financial reporting. Maintain a robust internal control environment and ensure timely completion of annual audits by independent auditors to provide assurance on the financial statements.
