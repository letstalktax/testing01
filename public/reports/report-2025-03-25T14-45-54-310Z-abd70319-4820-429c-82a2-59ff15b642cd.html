7. Related Party Transactions

Note 32 of the Example Financial Statements addresses related party transactions. UAE Corporate Tax Law, under Article 34, mandates that transactions with Related Parties must adhere to the arm's length principle.

Table 4: Related Party Transaction Considerations for UAE CT

Aspect UAE CT Compliance
Arm's Length Principle All transactions with related parties must be conducted at market value to ensure fair Taxable Income calculation.
Disclosure (Note 32) While the example discloses related party transactions as per IFRS, specific disclosures may be required under UAE CT law, particularly for transfer pricing documentation.
Documentation Maintain thorough documentation to support the arm's length nature of related party transactions, as required by Article 55 of the UAE Corporate Tax Law.

Companies in the UAE must ensure that all related party transactions are properly documented and priced at arm's length to comply with UAE CT regulations.

8. Actionable Recommendations

  1. IFRS Compliance Review: Conduct a thorough review of financial reporting processes to ensure full compliance with IFRS and its latest amendments, as mandated by UAE CT Law.
  2. Going Concern Documentation: Strengthen going concern assessments, explicitly documenting the consideration of economic uncertainties and their potential impacts on the business.
