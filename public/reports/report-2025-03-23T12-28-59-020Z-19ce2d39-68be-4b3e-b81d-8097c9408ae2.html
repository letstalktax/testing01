5.3 Potential Corporate Tax Adjustments & Considerations

5.3.1 Related Party Transactions

Note 14 highlights significant related party balances and transactions. These require detailed scrutiny under Article 34 of the UAE Corporate Tax Law to ensure compliance with the Arm's Length Principle. Potential Transfer Pricing risks may arise if transactions are not conducted at Market Value.

Action Required:

  • Conduct a thorough review of all related party transactions, including intercompany customer receivables and payables.
  • Prepare detailed Transfer Pricing documentation, including functional analysis, comparability analysis, and selection of the most appropriate Transfer Pricing method to demonstrate arm's length pricing.
  • Ensure all related party transactions are supported by intercompany agreements and are aligned with the Arm's Length Principle.

5.3.2 Depreciation & Amortization

Note 4 and 6 indicate depreciation and amortization expenses. Review of depreciation and amortization policies is necessary to ensure compliance with UAE Corporate Tax Law, particularly regarding rates and methods used.

Action Required:

  • Review the company's depreciation and amortization policies for all asset classes.
  • Ensure that the rates and methods used are compliant with generally accepted accounting principles and are justifiable for Corporate Tax purposes.
  • Maintain detailed fixed asset registers and amortization schedules for audit trail and documentation purposes.

5.3.3 Finance Charges

Note 20 indicates finance charges. Analysis is required to determine the deductibility of finance charges under Article 27 of the UAE Corporate Tax Law, including the General Interest Deduction Limitation Rule (Article 30).

Action Required:

  • Assess applicability of the General Interest Deduction Limitation Rule and prepare necessary calculations and documentation.
  • Ensure proper documentation for all finance arrangements, including loan agreements and lease contracts.

5.3.4 Accrued Expenses

Note 15 indicates accrued expenses and other payables. Review of accrued expenses is necessary to ensure that they are deductible under UAE Corporate Tax Law, specifically that they are incurred wholly and exclusively for the business and are not capital in nature (Article 28).

Action Required:

  • Conduct a detailed review of accrued expenses to ensure deductibility under UAE Corporate Tax Law.
  • Verify that accrued expenses are supported by sufficient documentation and relate to the current tax period's business activities.
  • Establish a process for regular review and verification of accrued expenses.

5.3.5 Loss Carry Forward

Gourmet Gulf Co LLC incurred a net loss for the year. As per Article 37 of the UAE Corporate Tax Law, Tax Losses can be carried forward to offset future Taxable Income, subject to conditions.

Action Required:

  • Track and document the Tax Losses incurred in the current tax period.
  • Ensure proper records are maintained for carry forward and utilization of Tax Losses in future tax periods, in accordance with Article 37 of the UAE Corporate Tax Law.
  • Monitor for any changes in ownership that may impact the ability to carry forward Tax Losses.
