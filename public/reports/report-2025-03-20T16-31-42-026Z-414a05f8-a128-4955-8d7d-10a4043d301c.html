4. Tax Implications

4.1. Corporate Tax

The UAE introduced corporate tax on business profits effective for financial years starting on or after 1 June 2023. While these financial statements are for the year ending 31 December 2023, the applicability of corporate tax for Gourmet Gulf Co. LLC in 2023 would depend on the start date of their financial year. Given the net loss reported for 2023, it is unlikely that the company will have a corporate tax liability for this period. However, it is crucial to monitor future profitability and ensure compliance with corporate tax regulations as the business evolves.

4.2. Value Added Tax (VAT)

As a restaurant business in the UAE, Gourmet Gulf Co. LLC is subject to VAT. The presence of 'VAT payable' in the accrued expenses indicates that the company is registered for VAT and is likely compliant with VAT regulations. Continued adherence to VAT rules, including accurate record-keeping and timely filing of returns, is essential.

4.3. Transfer Pricing

The financial statements reveal significant transactions and balances with related parties. Note 14 highlights substantial 'Due from related parties' and 'Due to a related party' balances. It is imperative that these related party transactions are conducted at arm's length to comply with potential transfer pricing regulations and to ensure that transactions are commercially justifiable. Proper documentation should be maintained to support the arm's length nature of these transactions.
